Profile

Professor Joseph Ifeanyichukwu Ikechebelu is a distinguished academic and medical professional specializing in Obstetrics and Gynaecology at Nnamdi Azikiwe University. He has made significant contributions to the fields of Gynecological Endoscopy, Infertility & Assisted Reproduction, and Prevention of Mother-To-Child Transmission (PMTCT). He is a pioneer in reproductive health, endoscopic surgery and HIV prevention research. Driven by excellence, he blends clinical expertise with academic leadership to shape the future of healthcare and higher education in Nigeria.

Research & Publications

I specialize in Reproductive Health, Endoscopy, and Maternal Care.

Professor Ikechebelu's research focuses on advancing minimally invasive surgical techniques in gynecology. With an extensive publication record with over 170 journal articles, 90 of which are listed in PubMed. His work has garnered over 3,500 citations, reflecting his significant impact in the field.   • Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission of HIV in South-East Nigeria

    This study evaluates the effectiveness of antiretroviral therapy and counseling in reducing HIV transmission from pregnant women to their babies.

  • Laparoscopic Gynecologic Surgery in Nigeria

    A pioneering work documenting the early experience and outcomes of minimally invasive gynecologic surgeries at Life International Hospital, Awka.

  • Infertility in Southeastern Nigeria: A Hospital-Based Study

    This publication explores the prevalence, causes, and sociocultural impact of infertility among couples in southeastern Nigeria.

  • Awareness and Acceptability of HPV Vaccine Among Mothers

    Investigates maternal knowledge and willingness to vaccinate their daughters against Human Papillomavirus in Anambra State

  • An Evaluation of Intrauterine Insemination Success Rates in a Nigerian Fertility Center

    A statistical review of outcomes and factors influencing success in assisted reproductive techniques in Nigeria.

  • Hysteroscopic Management of Intrauterine Adhesions

    Details the surgical management and recovery outcomes for women with Asherman’s Syndrome using hysteroscopic techniques.
